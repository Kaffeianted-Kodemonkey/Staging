Frequently Asked Questions

  • Can web accessibility improve SEO?

    Yes, web accessibility can improve SEO. When websites are designed and developed with accessibility in mind, they become more user-friendly for all users, including those with disabilities. This can lead to a better user experience, increased engagement, and higher conversion rates, which are all factors that search engines like Google take into account when ranking websites.

    Furthermore, many accessibility practices, such as providing descriptive alt text for images, using semantic HTML, and improving page load speed, also have a positive impact on SEO. These practices help search engines better understand the content on a website and can lead to higher search engine rankings.

    In addition, making a website accessible can increase its reach and audience. When a website is accessible, it can be used by people with a wide range of disabilities, including visual, auditory, and motor disabilities. This can help increase traffic to the website, which can improve its SEO.

    Overall, making a website accessible can improve its SEO, as it can lead to a better user experience, increased engagement, higher conversion rates, and better search engine rankings.

  • Why you should add accessibility now and not later?

    There are several reasons why it is important to prioritize web accessibility now rather than later:

    1) Legal requirements: Depending on your location and the industry you operate in, you may be legally required to ensure that your website is accessible to all users. Delaying accessibility efforts can leave you vulnerable to legal action.

    2) Inclusive design: By making your website accessible, you are ensuring that all users, regardless of their abilities or disabilities, can access and use your content. This can improve the user experience for everyone and help you reach a wider audience.

    3) Cost savings: Addressing accessibility issues early in the development process can save you time and money in the long run. Retrofitting an inaccessible website can be much more expensive and time-consuming than building an accessible site from the start.

    4) Brand reputation: Prioritizing accessibility sends a message to your users that you value inclusivity and are committed to making your content accessible to all. This can improve your brand reputation and help you attract and retain customers.

    In summary, adding accessibility to your website now is essential to ensure legal compliance, improve user experience for all users, save costs in the long run, and enhance your brand reputation.

  • What is ADA Compliance?

    ADA compliance refers to meeting the requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990, which prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in all areas of public life, including employment, education, transportation, and public accommodations.

    In the context of the web, ADA compliance means ensuring that websites, mobile applications, and other digital content are accessible to individuals with disabilities, such as those who are blind or have low vision, deaf or hard of hearing, or have mobility impairments. This includes making sure that web content can be navigated, understood, and interacted with using assistive technologies such as screen readers, keyboard-only navigation, and captions or transcripts for audio and video content.

    The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 are the internationally recognized standards for web accessibility, and meeting these guidelines is the key to achieving ADA compliance. The guidelines provide a set of success criteria and techniques for making web content accessible to individuals with disabilities. By following these guidelines, web developers and designers can ensure that their digital content is accessible to everyone, including people with disabilities, and avoid legal liability under the ADA regulations.

  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973

    Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 is a federal law in the United States that requires federal agencies to make their electronic and information technology accessible to people with disabilities. The law was enacted to ensure that people with disabilities have the same access to information and communication technologies as those without disabilities.

    Under Section 508, federal agencies are required to comply with accessibility standards for electronic and information technology. These standards are known as the "Section 508 Standards" and cover a range of technologies, including websites, software, hardware, and multimedia.

    The Section 508 Standards were updated in 2017 to align with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.0, which are international accessibility guidelines for web content. The updated standards also expanded the types of technologies covered to include mobile devices, social media, and other emerging technologies.

    Compliance with Section 508 is important for ensuring that people with disabilities have equal access to government services and information. It also sets an example for other organizations to follow in making their digital content accessible to all users.
